The role

This is not a generic designer job.

Your job is to sit at the front of the creative pipeline. Strategists bring angles, hooks, and briefs. You turn them into striking AI-generated image directions. Designers then composite the best raw imagery into final ads.

If your portfolio looks like generic AI stock art, this is not the role. If you can make a strange, funny, sharp visual metaphor that supports a direct-response hook, we want to see your work.

How the work flows

Every ad starts with a visual direction.

We care about speed, taste, range, and clean handoff. The goal is not to make one beautiful image. The goal is to create enough strong raw concepts that the final ad has a real chance of winning.

01

Brief

You receive an angle, hook, audience, and brand context.

02

Concept

You pitch several visual directions before generating volume.

03

Generate

You produce 12-18 images per brief with controlled variation.

04

Iterate

You refine quickly from strategist feedback without ego.

05

Handoff

You organize selected outputs, prompt notes, and designer direction.

What good work feels like

We want visual concepts with a point of view.

A useful AI image is not just polished. It makes the ad angle easier to understand, harder to ignore, or more emotionally obvious.

Sample direction
A full calendar is not the same as a full pipeline.
Brief

“Your calendar is full but your sales calls are wasting your time.”

Weak direction

A generic coach smiling at a laptop with floating calendar icons.

Strong direction

A packed calendar visually leaking low-quality leads, with one qualified call highlighted like a signal in the noise.

What we care about

Contrast, metaphor, composition, and whether a designer could turn the raw image into a real ad.

What you'll do

You bring the first visual spark.

Generate commercial-grade imagery

Use Midjourney, Flux, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion, Higgsfield, Nano Banana, or similar tools to create raw visual assets for static ads.

Push unexpected concepts

Humor, contrast, surrealism, metaphors, pattern breaks. We want visual ideas that make people pause, not safe stock-photo compositions.

Collaborate with strategists

Move fast in real-time feedback loops. Understand the marketing point, then make the visual sharper with each round.

Work at production pace

Once ramped, you should be comfortable with 4 briefs per day and 12-18 generated images per brief.

Build reusable systems

Track prompts, selected outputs, reference images, and tool notes so the team can reuse what works.

Stay AI-forward

Test new models, workflows, and techniques before they become obvious. Bring the team better ways to generate stronger creative.

Good fit / bad fit

This role is for AI-native visual thinkers.

You might be a fit if...

  • You already use AI image tools every week for real work.
  • You can explain why an image works for an ad.
  • You enjoy high-volume visual exploration.
  • You organize prompts and outputs cleanly.
  • You can take feedback without getting defensive.

You probably are not a fit if...

  • Your work looks like generic AI stock imagery.
  • You only want to make final polished layouts.
  • You need perfect briefs before starting.
  • You repeat the same style for every concept.
  • You cannot work consistent Eastern Time hours.
